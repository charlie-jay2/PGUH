Community and Staff Safety at Prince George University Hospital

Published: August 28, 2025 • By Trust Executives Team

At Prince George University Hospital (PGUH), the safety and wellbeing of both our community and our staff team remain our top priority. As part of the NHS Roleplay Trust, we are committed to ensuring that our hospital continues to provide a safe, supportive, and inclusive environment for everyone.

For Our Community

  • Respectful Roleplay – All patients, visitors, and players are expected to roleplay respectfully and responsibly. Harassment, abuse, or any form of disruptive behaviour will not be tolerated.
  • Safe Spaces – Designated safe areas are available for roleplay breaks and for anyone who needs time away from active scenarios.
  • Zero Tolerance Policy – Any player engaging in threats, discrimination, or unsafe behaviour will be removed to protect the wider community.

For Our Staff

  • Professional Standards – Our staff team, from clinical roles to security, are trained to uphold the highest standards of roleplay professionalism.
  • Support & Reporting – Staff have access to clear reporting pathways if they feel unsafe or encounter issues during their duties.
  • Wellbeing First – We encourage all team members to take breaks, balance their roleplay time, and place their wellbeing above all else.

Our Commitment

PGUH will continue to work with both the community and staff to uphold an environment of trust, safety, and respect. Every update, training session, and system improvement will be made with safety in mind — ensuring our hospital remains a welcoming place for all.

“Together, we can make PGUH a model of excellence within the NHS Trust”

If you have concerns or suggestions about community and staff safety, please contact a member of our leadership team.
